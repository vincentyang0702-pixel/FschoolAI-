## Part 2: What Data Do We Need?

### The 8 Data Dimensions

#### **Dimension 1: Neural Efficiency Data**
**What to collect:**
- Response time (speed)
- Accuracy (correctness)
- Pattern recognition success (can they identify patterns?)
- Transfer success (can they apply to new contexts?)

**How to collect:**
- Time-stamp each action
- Track accuracy of predictions
- Track pattern recognition in exercises
- Track transfer in new contexts

**Data points per student per day:** 50-100

#### **Dimension 2: Learning Modality Data**
**What to collect:**
- Performance with visual content
- Performance with auditory content
- Performance with kinesthetic content
- Performance with linguistic content

**How to collect:**
- Present content in different modalities
- Track performance in each
- Measure engagement in each
- Measure retention in each

**Data points per student per day:** 20-40

#### **Dimension 3: Emotional State Data**
**What to collect:**
- Engagement level (high/low)
- Confidence level (high/low)
- Motivation level (high/low)
- Stress level (high/low)

**How to collect:**
- Self-report (quick surveys)
- Behavioral signals (click patterns, hesitation)
- Physiological signals (if available - heart rate, etc.)
- Outcome signals (success/failure)

**Data points per student per day:** 30-60

#### **Dimension 4: Metacognitive Data**
**What to collect:**
- Self-awareness (do they know what they know?)
- Strategy awareness (do they know their learning strategy?)
- Learning awareness (do they know how they learn?)
- Metacognitive accuracy (how accurate is their self-assessment?)

**How to collect:**
- Ask "Do you understand this?"
- Compare to actual performance
- Track accuracy of self-assessment
- Measure improvement over time

**Data points per student per day:** 20-40

#### **Dimension 5: Transfer Data**
**What to collect:**
- Transfer success (can they apply to new contexts?)
- Abstraction ability (can they abstract principles?)
- Novel application (can they create new applications?)
- Context flexibility (can they work in different contexts?)

**How to collect:**
- Present learned concept in new context
- Measure success
- Measure abstraction level
- Measure novelty of application

**Data points per student per day:** 10-20

#### **Dimension 6: Social Learning Data**
**What to collect:**
- Collaboration preference (do they prefer group or individual?)
- Teaching ability (can they teach others?)
- Peer learning effectiveness (do they learn from peers?)
- Social anxiety (are they comfortable in social contexts?)

**How to collect:**
- Offer collaborative vs. individual options
- Track choice
- Measure learning in each context
- Measure comfort level

**Data points per student per day:** 10-20

#### **Dimension 7: Motivation Data**
**What to collect:**
- Extrinsic motivation (do they respond to rewards?)
- Intrinsic motivation (do they want to learn?)
- Purpose-driven motivation (do they see meaning?)
- Mastery-driven motivation (do they want to improve?)

**How to collect:**
- Offer different reward types
- Track engagement with each
- Measure persistence
- Measure goal-setting

**Data points per student per day:** 20-40

#### **Dimension 8: Cognitive Style Data**
**What to collect:**
- Analytical thinking (can they break down?)
- Intuitive thinking (can they see patterns?)
- Sequential thinking (do they prefer step-by-step?)
- Holistic thinking (do they prefer big picture?)

**How to collect:**
- Present problems in different ways
- Track success with each approach
- Measure preference
- Measure effectiveness

**Data points per student per day:** 20-40

### Total Data Collection

**Per student per day:** 180-360 data points
**Per student per month:** 5,400-10,800 data points
**Per 100 students per month:** 540,000-1,080,000 data points

**This is manageable and provides rich personalization.**

#### **Metric 6: Metacognitive Accuracy**

#### **Metric 7: Social Learning Effectiveness**
